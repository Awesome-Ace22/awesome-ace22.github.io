Project Description

The Autonomous Battlebot project was developed for participation in the Robowars competition. Robowars is an exciting competition where innovation, engineering, and competitive spirit collide. Participants design and build autonomous or remotely controlled robots to engage in intense battles. This event, hosted annually,provides a platform for both amateur enthusiasts and seasoned engineers to showcase their creativity and technical expertise. It emphasizes problem-solving, mechanical design, programming, and strategic thinking, making it a thrilling experience for participants and spectators alike.

The objective was to design and build a fully autonomous robot capable of competing in a sumo-style battle, where the goal is to push the opponent's robot out of the arena. The project involved extensive mechanical and electrical design, as well as programming to ensure autonomous operation.

Experience Completing the Project

This project was a challenging and rewarding experience that required a multidisciplinary approach. The design process included creating detailed sketches and CAD models, developing custom PCBs, and programming the robot's control system. The competition rules and constraints, such as size and weight limits, added an extra layer of complexity to the design process.

The initial design phase involved creating rough sketches to conceptualize the robot's structure and mechanisms. This step was crucial for visualizing the overall design and identifying potential challenges early on.

The CAD models provided a detailed and precise representation of the robot, allowing for thorough analysis and optimization of the design. These models were essential for ensuring that all components fit together correctly and that the robot met the competition's specifications.

Custom PCBs were designed to manage the robot's power distribution and control signals. This involved selecting appropriate components, designing the circuit layout, and ensuring reliable connections.

The final CAD images showcased the completed design, highlighting the robot's compact and robust structure. These images were used to guide the assembly process and ensure that the physical build matched the design specifications.
