Project Description

In our second year of engineering, my MIE243 class was given the challenge of designing a beginner-friendly CNC machine tailored for hobbyist machinists. The aim was to address a gap in the market: a reliable, low-maintenance, and affordable CNC router with adequate precision for home-based projects involving wood, plastic, and aluminum. After extensive research, benchmarking, and iterative design processes, my team and I produced a functional and cost-effective design aligned with user needs. This project allowed us to explore engineering design rigor while integrating real-world considerations such as material selection, manufacturability, and usability.
