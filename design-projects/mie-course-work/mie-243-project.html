Project Description

In our second year of engineering, my MIE243 class was given the challenge of designing a beginner-friendly CNC machine tailored for hobbyist machinists. The aim was to address a gap in the market: a reliable, low-maintenance, and affordable CNC router with adequate precision for home-based projects involving wood, plastic, and aluminum. After extensive research, benchmarking, and iterative design processes, my team and I produced a functional and cost-effective design aligned with user needs. This project allowed us to explore engineering design rigor while integrating real-world considerations such as material selection, manufacturability, and usability.

Experience and Key Learnings

Completing this project was an enriching experience that honed my ability to work collaboratively in a multidisciplinary setting. From conceptualization to final design, the iterative design approach fostered critical thinking and problem-solving. Balancing cost constraints with technical specifications offered insights into the trade-offs inherent in engineering design.

Key components included:

  • Developing CAD models to simulate design performance.
  • Exploring different motor systems and mechanical components to optimize performance.
  • Refining the prototype to ensure it met safety, reliability, and ease-of-use criteria.

The preliminary sketch was the first step in visualizing the design. It helped in identifying the basic structure and components required for the CNC machine.

The final CAD design provided a detailed and precise representation of the CNC machine. This model was crucial for simulating mechanical performance and validating the design before manufacturing.

Engineering drawings were generated to guide the manufacturing process. These drawings included detailed specifications and dimensions for each component.
